Current research

Research

My research spans computational fluid dynamics in porous and complex media, with numerical modelling as the common thread.

01

Lattice Boltzmann Methods

D3Q19 pore-scale hydrodynamics coupled to advection–diffusion–reaction models, with verification through permeability, mass conservation, and breakthrough behaviour.

  • D3Q19
  • Pe / Da
  • CPU / GPU
02

Rock Acidification

Coupled HCl transport, surface kinetics, and pore-geometry evolution to analyse compact, wormholing, and uniform dissolution regimes in carbonates.

  • HCl
  • Kinetics
  • PVBT
03

Porous-Media Dynamics

Micro-CT segmentation, connectivity and skeleton analysis, pore-scale flow, and structure–property relations for heterogeneous digital rocks.

  • Micro-CT
  • Connectivity
  • Permeability

Research interests

Exploring how fluids move through complex media

I explore a broad range of topics rather than claiming specialist status in each one. My core expertise is computational modelling of fluid dynamics.

01

Fluid Dynamics & Porous Media

Lattice Boltzmann, Darcy–Brinkman, Forchheimer, and pore-network simulations for flow across scales.

02

Complex Domains & Mesh Refinement

Local and adaptive mesh refinement, grid-independence studies, and numerical representation of irregular geometries.

03

Multiphase & Reactive Flow

Oil–brine systems, transport, interfacial phenomena, mineral reactions, and evolving porous structures.

04

Scientific Machine Learning

Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) applied to physical systems and porous media.

05

Hydrates & Emerging Problems

Computational investigation of hydrates and other coupled fluid-flow problems in geophysical and energy contexts.
